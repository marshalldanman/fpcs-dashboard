# Rhyme Toolkit

Reference palette for the Rhyme & Meter Savant. The floor constraint is absolute: **standard AABB and ABAB end-rhyme schemes are rejected.** Weave the techniques below into every section, and give each section a **distinct rhyme footprint** so verse, chorus, and bridge feel sonically different.

## Techniques

### Multi-syllable end rhymes
Match the last 2–4 syllables, not just the final stressed vowel.
- *carry the weight / married the fate*
- *running on empty / coming up plenty*

### Internal rhymes
Place rhymes mid-line so the bar pays off twice.
- *I **stalled** in the **hall** while the city kept calling*
- *the **light** in your **eyes** writes a story tonight*

### Mosaic rhymes
Rhyme one word against a phrase of small words that sum to the same sound.
- *orange / door hinge*
- *silver / will you ever*
- *forgotten me / not honestly*

### Compound rhymes
Stack two rhymes back to back so the ear gets a double click.
- *broken open / spoken token*
- *low and slow / so it goes*

### Assonance chains
Repeat a vowel color across a line without strict rhyme — creates atmosphere rather than punctuation.
- *the **o**ld r**oa**d gl**o**ws under sm**o**ke*
- *cl**ea**n stre**e**ts, k**ee**p the be**a**t*

### Consonance chains
Mirror consonants across a line for grit and texture.
- *cracked black asphalt cracking back*

### Slant rhymes
Near-rhymes that keep momentum without sounding inevitable.
- *home / alone, *time / mine, *gone / wrong*

## Section Footprints

### Verse — tight internal rhymes
Conversational density. End rhymes can be slant or sparse; the **work happens mid-line**. Think dense, low-vowel, syllable-stacked. Lets the listener lean in.

### Pre-Chorus — accelerator
Shorten line lengths. Repeat a vowel to telegraph the chorus. Often a 2-line lift.

### Chorus — broad open vowels + compound end rhymes
Open the mouth: long *o*, *ay*, *eye*, *oh* sounds carry. End rhymes should **ring**, ideally compound or multi-syllable. The hook line should be the simplest, most singable rhyme of the song.

### Bridge — break the footprint
If verses were dense and internal, bridge goes spare and end-stopped. If chorus rang on long *o*, bridge pivots to short *i* or short *e*. The sonic shift signals the emotional pivot.

### Final Chorus — same words, re-colored
Lyric repetition with optional small lyric tweak (a single word swap that lands the resolution).

## Anti-Patterns

- Pure AABB couplets across the whole song.
- End-rhyming on the same single-syllable word twice (love/love).
- Forcing inversions to land a rhyme (*"the love I have for you so true"*).
- Cliché rhyme pairs: fire/desire, heart/apart, night/light, dance/romance — only use if subverted.
